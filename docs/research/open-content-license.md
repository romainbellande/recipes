# Open recipe-content licenses and attribution

## Scope

This note compares licenses for maintainer-authored Recipe prose, structured recipe data, and project-owned media. It does not license the Astro application code or third-party material, and is not legal advice.

## Findings

Creative Commons 4.0 is the applicable family for Recipe content. Creative Commons recommends against using CC licenses for software, so the repository should clearly separate Recipe content from application code and any third-party material.

### CC BY 4.0

CC BY permits copying, sharing, adapting, and commercial use. Public reusers must retain supplied creator, copyright, license, disclaimer, and source-URI information where reasonably practicable; link or provide the license; and identify modifications. It does not require a reuser to publish an adaptation or its editable source.

### CC BY-SA 4.0

CC BY-SA has the same attribution and commercial-use permissions as CC BY. When an adaptation is shared, the adapter must license their contribution under CC BY-SA (or an approved compatible license) and cannot add restrictions or DRM. It does not require publication of private adaptations or editable source files.

### CC0 1.0

CC0 waives copyright and related rights to the extent permitted, with a broad fallback license. It maximizes reuse, including commercial reuse, but imposes no enforceable attribution or share-alike requirement.

### Less direct alternatives

GFDL has document-specific preservation and, for large opaque distributions, transparent-copy obligations. ODC-By governs database rights but does not independently license copyright in individual Recipe content. Neither is as direct a fit for Recipe prose as Creative Commons.

## Attribution and scope guidance

If CC BY or CC BY-SA is chosen, publish a notice naming the project or author, linking the canonical Recipe or repository, naming and linking the license, and asking reusers to identify modifications. Brand names and trademarks remain outside the license. Explicitly identify which paths contain project-owned Recipe content and which use a separate software license or have third-party rights.

## Remaining decision

Choose one of:

- **CC BY 4.0** for attribution with the least downstream licensing constraint.
- **CC BY-SA 4.0** for attribution plus reciprocal licensing of shared adaptations.
- **CC0 1.0** for maximum reuse without enforceable attribution.

The decision should also establish the project attribution identity, canonical URL, and the repository paths that are covered.
